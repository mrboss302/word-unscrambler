What Is an Anagram?

An anagram is a word made by rearranging the letters of another word or phrase, using each letter exactly once. Listen and silent are classic anagrams. A sub-anagram uses only some of the letters — for example, parse from the letters in spare.

Full anagrams versus sub-anagrams

There are two kinds of answer this tool produces, and knowing the difference helps you read the results. A full anagram uses every letter of your input exactly once — listen and silent are full anagrams because both use the same seven letters. A sub-anagram uses only some of the letters, like tin or nest from those same seven. Full anagrams are the prize; sub-anagrams are the supporting cast that often matter more in actual games, where you rarely need to spend every tile.

Because the generator returns both and groups them by length, the longest group is where any full anagrams will appear, and the shorter groups give you the playable sub-anagrams.

How to solve an anagram by hand

When you want to crack one yourself, a simple method beats staring. First, separate the vowels from the consonants — seeing how many vowels you have and where they can sit narrows the field quickly. Next, look for common building blocks: beginnings like st-, tr-, ch-, and un-, and endings like -ing, -ed, -er, -ly, and -tion. Park a likely ending, then arrange the remaining letters in front of it. If you hit a wall, physically rewrite the letters in a new random order; breaking the original sequence stops your brain from re-reading the same non-word. The generator is there for when you simply want the answer, or to check that you found them all.

Anagrams worth knowing

Part of the fun of anagrams is how often the rearrangement seems to comment on the original. A few classics: dormitory rearranges to dirty room; the eyes becomes they see; and astronomer hides moon starer. None of these are coincidences you would spot by reading left to right — they only appear once you pool the letters and look again, which is exactly what the generator does for you.

Fun ways to use the anagram generator

Beyond solving puzzles, anagrams are a quick source of creative names. Drop in your own name to find a hidden word or a pen-name, mix a team's player names into a club handle, or feed in a project title to look for a memorable username. Writers use anagrams to build pseudonyms and puzzle setters use them to design fair clues.
