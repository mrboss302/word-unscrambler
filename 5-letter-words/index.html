How to Find 5-Letter Words

  1. Add a filter. Enter starting letters, ending letters, or letters the word should contain.
  2. Include or exclude letters. Require letters anywhere in the word, or rule letters out.
  3. Try a pattern. Lock known positions with a pattern like A__E_.
  4. Find words. Select Find 5-Letter Words for an alphabetical list of matches.

5-Letter Words Starting With

Use the “starts with” field to list five-letter words by their opening letters. For example, st surfaces words like stone, start, and steam, while a single letter like c returns every five-letter word that begins with C.

5-Letter Words Ending In

The “ends with” field filters by suffix. Try e for words like spare, trace, and crane, or a two-letter ending such as er to narrow the list further.

5-Letter Words With Specific Letters

Use “includes letters” to keep only words that contain the letters you specify, and “excludes letters” to drop words that contain letters you have ruled out. A letter you both include and exclude — which can happen with repeated letters — is kept, so your known letters always win.

Useful groups of five-letter words

Five-letter words reward a little memorization, because the same handy sets keep coming up across Wordle, crosswords, and Scrabble. A few groups are worth keeping in your back pocket.

Vowel-heavy words

When your rack or your guess is swimming in vowels, words like adieu, audio, queue, ourie, raise, arose, and arise turn a liability into a play. Vowel-rich openers are also popular in Wordle because they test several vowels at once.

Words that spend rare letters

High-value tiles are easier to use when you know words that absorb them: jazzy, fuzzy, pizza, zesty, zebra, jumbo, joker, quart, queen, extra, and waltz all pack a J, Q, X, or Z into five letters. Use the includes letters field to pull up more of them on demand.

Words with no repeated letters

For a Wordle opener you usually want five different letters so no slot is wasted. Plenty of common words qualify — crane, slate, trace, adieu, and horse among them. The exclude field is handy here for ruling out letters you have already eliminated.

Five-letter patterns worth knowing

Many five-letter words cluster around a handful of shapes. Recognizing them helps you guess the rest of a word from just a couple of letters. Strong endings include -ound (round, sound, pound, mound, found), -ight (light, might, night, sight, right), and -ough (rough, tough, dough, cough, bough). Common openings include st- (stone, start, steam, stair) and ch- (chair, chest, charm, chase). To explore any of these, drop the ending into the ends with field or the opening into starts with, and the finder lists the full set.

Why five-letter words matter

Five letters is the sweet spot of word games. It is the exact length of a Wordle answer, one of the most common slot lengths in a crossword grid, and a reliable mid-length Scrabble play that balances points against the tiles it uses. Building a feel for five-letter words pays off across all three, which is why this finder keeps its search fixed at five and lets you slice it by starting letters, ending letters, and the letters in between.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find 5-letter words?

Add any filters you like — starts with, ends with, contains, include, exclude, or a pattern — then select Find 5-Letter Words.

Can I search for 5-letter words starting with a letter?

Yes, with the starts-with field, e.g. st.

Can I search for 5-letter words ending in a letter?

Yes, with the ends-with field, e.g. e.

Can I use this for Wordle?

Yes. For clue-based filtering, the Wordle helper adds green, yellow, and gray inputs.

Are all 5-letter words accepted by every game?

No. The list is a broad English word list and may include obscure words. It may not match every official game dictionary, and Word Puzzle Solver is not affiliated with any game publisher — confirm a word with your game’s official dictionary if needed. See the dictionary and word list page.
