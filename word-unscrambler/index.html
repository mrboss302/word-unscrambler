How to use the word unscrambler

What is a word unscrambler?

A word unscrambler takes a set of mixed-up letters and finds the valid words hiding inside them. Instead of rearranging tiles in your head, you hand the letters to the tool and it checks them against a dictionary, returning every word you can actually spell.

It is the fastest way to break a tough rack in Scrabble, find a play in Words With Friends, test guesses in Wordle, or crack the daily Jumble. Because everything runs in your browser, results appear instantly and keep working even if your connection drops.

Tips for finding better words

Start with your longest options

Results are sorted longest-first for a reason: longer words usually score more and clear more tiles. Scan the top groups before settling for a short play.

Watch your high-value letters

How a word unscrambler actually works

Behind the simple box, the unscrambler does something you could do by hand — just much faster. First it cleans your input down to letters, ignoring spaces, numbers, and symbols. Then it counts how many of each letter you have, turning a rack like LETTERS into a tally: one L, one R, one S, two T's, and two E's. That tally is the key to the whole process.

To decide whether a dictionary word is playable, the tool checks that word's own letter tally against yours. A word qualifies only if you hold at least as many of every letter it needs. This is why repeated letters matter so much: the rack above can spell letter because it has the two T's and two E's that word requires, but a rack with a single E could not. Most online frustration with unscramblers comes from this rule — you cannot reuse a tile you do not physically have.

A worked example: the rack SAILORS

Type SAILORS and the tool counts two S's, one A, one I, one L, one O, and one R. It then sweeps the dictionary and keeps every word that fits inside that tally — a word like airfoils fails because there is no F in your rack, but sailor, solar, lasso, sisal, silo, also, and many shorter words pass. Results arrive grouped from longest to shortest, because in most games the longest word clears the most tiles and scores the most points. Each result carries its Scrabble value, so a quick glance tells you whether also or oar is the better dump.

Reading your results well

Scan the longest group first — a seven-letter result on a seven-tile rack is a Scrabble bingo and a 50-point bonus. If nothing long fits the board, drop to the shorter groups for a play that slots into a tight space or sets up your next turn. Use the must include field when a board letter has to appear in your word, and the length filter when you are solving a puzzle that demands an exact word length.

Where unscrambling helps beyond Scrabble

Rearranging letters is useful well outside the Scrabble board. It cracks the daily newspaper Jumble, where the whole puzzle is an anagram. It helps anyone learning English spelling see which words a set of letters can form. Parents and teachers use it to build quick spelling drills from a child's name or this week's vocabulary. And puzzle setters use it in reverse — to check that a clever-looking jumble has a clean, single answer. Whatever the source of your letters, the method is the same: count them, then keep every word the count can build.

Frequently asked questions

What is a word unscrambler?

It is a tool that turns jumbled letters into valid words. You give it your letters, and it returns every word in its dictionary that you can build from them.

How does this word unscrambler work?

The tool counts the letters you enter, then checks each dictionary word to confirm you have enough of every letter to spell it. Matches are grouped by length and sorted longest-first. It all happens in your browser.

Can I use this for Scrabble?

Yes. Enter your rack to see playable words with their Scrabble scores, so you can choose the highest-value move.

Can I find 5-letter words for Wordle?

Yes. Set the length filter to 5 letters, and use the “must include” field to keep only words that contain a letter you have already confirmed.

Does the tool support blank tiles or wildcards?

Not yet — wildcard and blank-tile support is planned for a future update. For now, enter the exact letters you have.

Are all results valid in every word game?

Not necessarily. Results come from a broad English word list and may include obscure words, and they may not match every official game dictionary.
